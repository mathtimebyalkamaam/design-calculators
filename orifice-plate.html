Orifice Plate Flow Measurement Theory

Fundamental Principles

The orifice plate is one of most economical and widely used differential pressure flow measurement devices operating on Bernoulli's principle. As fluid accelerates through the orifice constriction, static pressure drops due to conversion of pressure energy to kinetic energy. The differential pressure is directly related to volumetric flow rate via fundamental fluid mechanics.

ISO 5167 Standard Compliance

ISO 5167-1:2022 establishes general principles and equipment requirements for differential pressure measurement; ISO 5167-2:2022 specifies orifice plate design, installation, and discharge coefficient calculations. Key parameters include beta ratio (d/D), Reynolds number, tap location (flange/vena contracta/corner), and orifice type (sharp-edged concentric, quadrant-edge, segmental). Proper adherence ensures measurement accuracy within ±2% at Reynolds numbers > 4000.

Discharge Coefficient Determination

The discharge coefficient (Cd) accounts for real-world deviations from ideal flow including vena contracta contraction, viscous effects, and flow separation. ISO 5167-2 provides detailed correlations: for sharp-edged orifices with flange taps, Cd depends on Reynolds number and beta ratio. At high Reynolds (>4000), Cd approaches approximately 0.61-0.65 regardless of beta. Careful calculation of Cd is essential for accurate flow measurement.

Compressibility Effects in Gases

For compressible fluids like air and steam, density changes across pressure drop, reducing measured flow compared to incompressible prediction. Expansibility factor accounts for this compressibility effect, reducing calculated flow by typically 1-10% for gas applications. Critical for accurate natural gas measurement in pipelines and industrial applications.

Installation and Operational Considerations

Proper installation is crucial: minimum 10D upstream and 4D downstream straight sections required to ensure fully developed flow. Pulsating flow, two-phase flow, or abnormal viscosity can significantly affect accuracy. Maintenance requires periodic cleaning to prevent blockage and erosion. Direct-acting manometers suitable for stable flow; for fluctuating conditions, damping or electronic differential pressure transmitters recommended.
